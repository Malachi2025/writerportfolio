The Weight of Still Water

A daughter returns to her childhood home after her mother’s death and discovers a cache of letters that rewrite everything she believed about her family — and herself. Set over a single Vermont autumn, the novel braids three women’s lives across four decades of silence and sacrifice.

Longlisted for the Women’s Prize for Fiction. New York Times Notable Book of the Year.

“A stunning, sorrowful work. Hace has written a novel about the stories families keep — and the cost of finally telling them.”

All the Borrowed Light

Set across three generations of a Cape Breton fishing family, this sweeping novel maps the cost of silence between men who love deeply and speak in riddles. Beginning in 1942, it follows three men named after the same grandfather, each inheriting his silences and his unspoken grief.

Finalist for the Dublin Literary Award. Sunday Times bestseller.

“Hace is doing something rare — building a mythology out of ordinary people, ordinary loss, ordinary love.”
— The Observer

PublisherPicador

A Kind of Leaving

A young woman vanishes from a remote Scottish village. What follows is not a mystery — it is an accounting of what it means to disappear by degrees, long before you are gone.

Longlisted for the Booker Prize. Winner of the Betty Trask Award for best debut Commonwealth novel.

“A remarkable debut. Spare, devastating, and utterly precise. Hace arrived as a fully formed writer.”
— Times Literary Supplement

What the Light Leaves Behind

Poetry Magazine · March 2024

New
There are mornings that do not end —
they fold themselves into the curtains,
into the particular weight of a cup
that someone else once held.

A meditation on the residue of memory and the way certain mornings refuse to leave us entirely.
