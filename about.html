Peter Hace

A life told
in sentences

Peter Hace grew up in a household where books were currency and storytelling was the native language. A graduate of the Iowa Writers’ Workshop, he spent his early career as a journalist before fiction pulled him back to the page with a force he couldn’t argue with.

His writing explores the spaces between people — the silences that accumulate into years, the small decisions that become entire lives. Critics have described his prose as “precise and devastating,” and his work has been translated into fourteen languages.

Beyond his own writing, Peter founded Spark. Write. Inspire. as a platform dedicated to emerging voices — a space where new writers can share their work and find the readers who are ready for them.

“Hace writes with the patience of someone who knows that the truth arrives late, and quietly.”
— The Guardian

He has taught creative writing at Columbia University and the Bread Loaf Writers’ Conference. His essays on craft have appeared in The Paris Review, Tin House, and The Atlantic. He lives between New York and the west coast of Ireland.
